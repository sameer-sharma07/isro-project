A B O U T

The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) is the esteemed space agency of the Government of India, founded in 1969 and headquartered in Bengaluru. With a mission to propel India's space research and exploration endeavors, ISRO has achieved remarkable milestones. It has successfully launched satellites for communication, remote sensing, navigation, and scientific research. Notably, ISRO's Mars Orbiter Mission (Mangalyaan) made India the first Asian nation to orbit Mars in 2014. The organization's lunar missions, such as Chandrayaan, have been vital for studying the Moon. Beyond space exploration, ISRO's satellites offer crucial services like weather forecasting and disaster management. Operating the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV) and the Geosynchronous Satellite Launch Vehicle (GSLV), ISRO has garnered global recognition for its cost-effective approach and innovation. Collaborating with international space agencies, ISRO continues to push the boundaries of space technology, seeking new knowledge and benefits for society.

Chandrayaan 1

Chandrayaan-1 was India's first lunar probe launched on October 22, 2008. It made significant discoveries, including confirming the presence of water molecules on the Moon's surface.

Chandrayan 2

Chandrayaan-2, launched in July 2019, was India's second lunar mission. It consisted of an orbiter, lander (Vikram), and rover (Pragyan), aiming to explore the Moon's south polar region.

see more

Chandrayan 3

Chandrayaan-3 is a follow-on to Chandrayaan-2 to demonstrate end-to-end capability in safe landing and roving on the lunar surface. The propulsion module will carry the lander and rover configuration till 100 km lunar orbit.

see more

mangalyaan

Mangalyaan, also known as the Mars Orbiter Mission , is India's first interplanetary mission launched by ISRO in November 2013. It successfully entered Mars' orbit, making India the first nation to reach Mars.

astrosat
